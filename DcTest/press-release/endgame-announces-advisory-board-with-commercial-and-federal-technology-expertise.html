Endgame Announces Advisory Board with Commercial and Federal Technology Expertise

Arlington, VA – February 20, 2014 – Endgame, Inc., a leading venture-backed provider of security intelligence and analytics solutions that give enterprises real-time visibility and actionable insight across their digital domains, today announced the formation of its Advisory Board. The Advisory Board members will lend their vision, range of experience, and extensive expertise in the commercial and federal technology space to Endgame’s leadership as the company continues to develop solutions that address a wide array of security problems for its federal and commercial customers.

“We are honored to have the opportunity to partner with such a distinguished group of individuals,” said Endgame CEO Nate Fick. “Each of our advisors brings unique expertise and a distinct perspective to Endgame, and I look forward to working with them as we continue to deepen our federal partnerships and expand into the enterprise market.”

Endgame Advisory Board members include:

Peter Bloom Peter Bloom is an Advisory Director for General Atlantic LLC. He served as a Managing Director of GA for 15 years before retiring in 2010. Mr. Bloom led global technology due diligence on prospective investments, provided technology and risk management assistance to CEOs and senior management teams of portfolio companies, and provided guidance on emerging technology trends for GA, its stakeholders, and several private and public entities. Mr. Bloom has served on several public, private, and non-profit boards.

Shelley Leibowitz Shelley Leibowitz served as the first Group-wide Chief Information Officer at the World Bank. In this role, she was responsible for the technology services and capabilities that underlie the work of delivering quality knowledge and financing products to the Bank Group’s clients across the globe. Previously, Ms. Leibowitz held Chief Information Officer roles at Investment Risk Management/Duff Capital Advisors, Morgan Stanley, Greenwich Capital Markets, and Barclays Capital. She served as Board member and technology advisor for GAIN Capital, a global provider of foreign exchange execution and clearing services with an award-winning trading platform.

Art Money The Honorable Arthur L. Money served as the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Command, Control, Communications and Intelligence from 1999 to 2001. Since leaving the Pentagon, Mr. Money has become a member of various USG Advisory Boards, Panels, and Commissions; a member of the Board of Directors of several U.S. public and private companies; and a member of several advisory boards. Mr. Money has more than 50 years of management and engineering experience with the defense electronics industry and intelligence community, specializing in the design and development of intelligence collection and analysis capabilities and airborne tactical reconnaissance systems.

Alan Wade Alan Wade retired from federal service at the end of 2005 after a thirty-five year career in the Central Intelligence Agency. Mr. Wade held a series of senior positions at the CIA, including Chief Information Officer, Director of Communications, and Director of Security. In his assignment as Chief Information Officer, he was dual-hatted as the Chief Information Officer for the United States Intelligence Community. He is a recipient of the National Intelligence Distinguished Service Medal, the Director’s Medal, and the Distinguished Intelligence Medal. Since retirement, Mr. Wade has worked with several early stage companies. He serves on public sector advisory boards of several technology companies, the advisory board of the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Virginia Tech, and the Board of the Organization of Retired CIA officers.

For more information about Endgame’s Advisory Board, visit www.endgame.com/about/#advisors.

About Endgame

Endgame is delivering the next generation of Security Intelligence & Analytics (SIA). Our core capabilities use data-science and cutting-edge technology to give our federal and commercial customers real-time visibility across their digital domains, and our ecosystem of applications use that insight to solve a wide array of security problems. Endgame allows you to see what others can’t, and to take control of your connected world.

Endgame was founded in 2008 and has offices in Washington DC, Atlanta, San Francisco and San Antonio. Endgame is backed by leading investors including Bessemer Venture Partners, Columbia Capital, Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers, Mithril Capital Management, and Paladin Capital Group.
